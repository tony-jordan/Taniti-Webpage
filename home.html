About Taniti!

Taniti is a small tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied, and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Taniti's economy primarily consists of tourism, with roots in fishing and agriculture.
Taniti has several options for your travel needs. Arrival can take place via air or ship, and the island has both public, and private ground transportation.

Guest Safety

The safety of travellers is a top priority on the island of Taniti. All lodging vendors are strictly regulated to ensure guest safety. Taniti also boasts a low crime rate. Most incidents arise due to low-level pickpocketing. While excursions into nature can be dangerous, organizations host very capable guides to provide vacationers' with safe and memorable experiences.
